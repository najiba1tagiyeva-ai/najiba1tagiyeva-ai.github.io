ENERGY TRANSITION ANALYSIS

Azerbaijan's Path Toward an Energy Transition

Exploring the shift from hydrocarbons to renewable energy

Understanding Azerbaijan's Energy Future

This comprehensive analysis explores Azerbaijan's journey from a hydrocarbon-dependent economy toward a sustainable energy future. Through data visualizations and regional comparisons, we examine the challenges, opportunities, and progress in renewable energy adoption.

1. Introduction

Azerbaijan is widely known for its dependence on oil and gas, but in recent years the country has announced ambitious plans to expand renewable energy and reduce emissions. As the global energy system changes, it is important to understand whether Azerbaijan is truly moving toward a sustainable energy transition. This project uses data from 1990–2024 to explore trends in energy production, carbon emissions, renewable investment, and regional comparison. Through a set of visualizations, it evaluates how far the country has progressed and what challenges remain.

2. Is Azerbaijan still oil-dependent?

Chart 1 – Energy production mix

Azerbaijan's energy landscape remains heavily dominated by fossil fuels despite global transition trends. Natural gas has emerged as the primary energy source, while oil production shows signs of long-term decline.

3. The carbon cost of growth

Chart 2 – CO₂ emissions vs GDP

Azerbaijan’s early post-Soviet years were highly carbon-intensive, with large CO₂ emissions despite low GDP. From the 2000s onward, the country achieved relative decoupling as GDP grew faster than emissions and carbon intensity steadily declined. However, the upward trend line shows that full decoupling has not yet occurred—economic growth still increases total CO₂ output.

8. Conclusion

Azerbaijan stands at a critical juncture in its energy history. While hydrocarbons continue to dominate the economy, significant opportunities exist for renewable energy development. Success will require sustained policy commitment, strategic investment, and regional cooperation. The transition presents both challenges and opportunities that will shape the country's economic and environmental future for decades to come.
